Kashi Vishwanath Mandir & Corridor

One of the 12 Jyotirlingas, the most sacred shrines of Lord Shiva.

  • Grants Moksha – Dying in Kashi is believed to break the cycle of rebirth.
  • Shiva as Vishwanath – Lord of the Universe.
  • “Kashi ke Kankar bhi Shankar hain” – Even the stones of Kashi are Shiva.

Architectural Style

  • North Indian Nagara architecture – Beehive-shaped shikhara
  • Gold-plated shikhara and silver sanctum
  • Mandapa: Pillared hall in front of sanctum

Main Sanctum (Garbhagriha)

  • Houses the self-manifested (Swayambhu) Lingam

    • Subsidiary Shrines:

  • Dedicated to Kaal Bhairav, Annapurna, Vishnu, Ganesha, Nandi

Shikhara (mandir Spire)

  • 51 ft tall, plated with 750 kg of golddonated by Maharaja Ranjit Singh
  • Made with sandstone and lime mortar

Mandir Layout

  • Garbhagriha: Inner sanctum housing the lingam.
  • Antarala: Vestibule between sanctum and mandapa.
  • Mandapa: Pillared hall for rituals.
  • Pradakshina Path: Circular path around sanctum.
  • Mukha Mandapa: Entrance hall filled with devotees.

Materials Used

  • Sandstone, Marble, Granite, wood, brass
  • Silver doors, gold spire, wooden carvings

Renovations & Corridor Expansion

  • Completed in 2021 – 24 new buildings added
  • Direct connection to River Ganga
  • 5 lakh sq. ft campus with pathways, ghats, and buildings
  • 300 meters from Manikarnika Ghat
